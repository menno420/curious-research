The temperature tower: one print that finds your best nozzle heat

A temperature tower is a single tall test print split into stacked bands, each printed at a different nozzle heat. You read the bands afterwards and pick the one that came out cleanest. Watch it, band by band.

What you just watched

A temperature tower is one tall print sliced into stacked bands, each printed a few degrees cooler than the one below it. Too hot and the plastic oozes — you get fine strings and blobs. Too cold and it won't fuse — weak layers, gaps, sagging bridges. Somewhere in the middle is a band that came out clean: solid layers, flat bridges, no hairs. That band's temperature is the one to use for that spool of filament — found in a single print instead of guessing.

Honest note — this is stylized. Real towers look different for every filament and every printer, and the numbers above (195–220 °C) are just an example range, not a recommendation — read the number on your spool. Temperature is not the only cause of stringing: retraction matters too — see the retraction-vs-stringing/ guide. And "good" temperatures vary by filament brand, so always trust what your tower shows you over any number you read online, including these.
